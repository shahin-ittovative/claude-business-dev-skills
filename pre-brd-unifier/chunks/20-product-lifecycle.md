# Product Lifecycle

The stages a product goes through from development and introduction to withdrawal from the market. It helps manage strategy, investment, marketing, and innovation over time. The stage focus is reference guidance; capture the product-specific plan and expected revenue posture per stage.

| Stage | Focus (reference) | Your product's activities & notes | Expected revenue posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Development | Conceptualizing, building, and testing the product. Validate real user needs and identify MVP scope that delivers clear value to all personas. |  |  |
| Introduction | Launching the product to the market; gain early traction and feedback through marketing, awareness, and onboarding early adopters. |  |  |
| Growth | Rapid adoption and increasing market share; capitalize on momentum by scaling infrastructure, improving features, and expanding channels. |  |  |
| Maturity | Maximizing profit and maintaining dominance; defend market position and extend lifecycle via optimization, differentiation, and retention. |  |  |
| Decline | Managing reduced demand; decide whether to phase out, replace, or reinvent the product (cost-cutting, retirement, or pivoting). |  |  |
